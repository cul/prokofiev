  TITLE: Les origines akkadiennes de Sept, ils sont sept de Serge Prokofiev

         ID: SPA_14027
         Periodical: Musicorum, Page(s) 185-201
         Publication date: 2005
         Language: French
         Call Number: spa-tex-per-0178
         Document type: Essay
         Periodical type: Journal
         Previous holding library: Serge Prokofiev Archive at Goldsmiths College, University of London
         Notes: (Abstract)On 4 September 1917, while he was
            finishing the orchestration of the Classical Symphony, Sergei Prokofiev began composing
            a now forgotten score: Seven, they are seven, an incantation for dramatic tenor, choir
            and orchestra. Prokofiev carefully analysed the poem by Konstantin Balmont, pointing out
            its salient aspects and designing the shape of its different parts. The composer was
            then able to sketch fragments of melodies and orchestral passages. The poem, thereby
            places in the centre of the creating process, has an unclear history dating from
            Mesopotamian High Antiquity, and deliberately played down by Balmont. It is thus a very
            narrow track that one has to follow in order to get the cuneiform Accadian inscription
            deciphered by Balmont, the evocative power of which was widely exploited by
            Prokofiev.
